People【人】 
擅长做计划的人 
Describe a person who makes plans a lot and is good at planning 
You should say: 
Who he or she is 
How you knew him or her 
What plans he or she makes 
And explain how you feel about this person 
 
教师的解析： 
审题方面 
这是一道“人物类+性格习惯类”话题，重点在于描述一个非常爱做计划的人。需要回答四点：这个人是谁、你如
何认识他或她、他或她通常做什么样的计划、以及你对这个人的看法和感受。时态以现在时为主，介绍认识过程时
可用过去时。 
思路方面 
可以选择身边真实的人，比如同学、朋友、同事或家人。重点不是“计划多复杂”，而是计划的频率和习惯，例如
学习计划、时间安排、旅行计划、日常生活清单等。最后要表达你对这种性格的态度，可以是欣赏、学习，或既喜
欢又觉得有压力。 
结构方面 
第一段：介绍这个人是谁，以及你是如何认识他或她的； 
第二段：具体说明他或她常做哪些计划，以及表现出来的习惯； 
第三段：表达你对这个人的看法和感受，并进行总结。 
 
答题参考 
背景描述： 
 
He is one of my closest friends. 他是我最亲近的朋友之一。 
I got to know him in high school. 我在高中认识他的。 
We have known each other for many years. 我们认识很多年了。 
She is the kind of person who likes order. 她是一个喜欢有条理的人。 
Planning is part of his daily life. 计划已经成为他日常生活的一部分。 
具体描述 
He makes study plans every week. 他每周都会制定学习计划。 
She writes down her tasks in a notebook. 她会把任务写在本子里。 
He plans trips in detail, including time and budget. 他会把旅行的时间和预算都计划好。 
She likes to prepare things in advance. 她喜欢提前准备事情。 
He rarely does things without a plan. 他几乎不做没有计划的事。 
描述感受 
I admire his self-discipline. 我很佩服他的自律。 
This habit makes him very reliable. 这个习惯让他非常可靠。 
Sometimes I feel pressured, but also motivated. 有时我有压力，但也被激励。 
I have learned to plan better from him. 我从他身上学会了更好地做计划。 
Overall, I respect this way of living. 总的来说，我很尊重这种生活方式。

Sample Answer  ： 
A person who makes plans a lot is my close friend David. I got to know him in high school, and we have stayed 
in touch ever since. From the very beginning, I noticed that he was very different from most people around me, 
because he always liked to arrange things in advance. 
David makes plans for almost everything in his life. He has weekly study plans, daily to-do lists, and even plans 
for weekends. For example, before an exam week, he writes down what subject to study each day and how 
much time to spend on it. When we travel together, he plans the schedule, transport, and even meal times. He 
also keeps reminders on his phone and a small notebook to track his progress. Because of this habit, he rarely 
feels rushed or confused. 
I have mixed but mostly positive feelings about this person. On the one hand, I really admire his self-discipline 
and organization. He is very reliable, and I feel safe working with him on group tasks. On the other hand, his 
detailed planning sometimes makes me feel a little pressured, because I am more relaxed by nature. However, I 
have learned a lot from him, especially how planning can save time and reduce stress. Overall, I respect his way 
of living, and I believe this habit will help him succeed in the future. 
 
Part 3  
Question 1: Do you think it’s important to plan ahead? 
解题思路：观点类问题。可以从“减少压力”“提高效率”“应对变化”来说明提前规划的重要性。 
Sample Answer: 
Yes, planning ahead is important. When people make plans, they know what to do and feel less stressed. 
Planning helps them use time better and avoid mistakes. 
For example, planning study or work tasks helps people finish things on time. Planning also prepares people for 
possible problems. 
 
Question 2: What activities do we need to plan ahead? 
解题思路：说明与原因类问题。可以从“活动复杂度高”“涉及多人或资源”“时间和费用成本高”这几个角度
展开，解释为什么有些活动如果不提前计划就容易出问题。重点放在“提前规划能解决什么问题”。 
Sample Answer: 
Many activities need to be planned ahead, especially those that involve time, money, or other people. Traveling 
is a good example. People need to book tickets, hotels, and plan routes, or the trip may become stressful. 
Big events like weddings, parties, or meetings also need planning. Without preparation, things may go wrong, 
such as missing items or schedule problems. 
Even study plans and work projects benefit from planning. Planning ahead helps people use time better, reduce 
stress, and feel more confident. 
 
Question 3: Do you think children should plan their future career? 
解题思路：观点类问题。重点不是“定死职业”，而是解释“早期思考”的好处，并说明不应给孩子太大压力。 
Sample Answer: 
I think children can think about their future career, but they should not plan it too strictly. Early thinking helps 
children understand their interests and strengths. It can also motivate them to study better. 
However, children are still growing, and their interests may change. If parents push them too hard, children 
may feel stressed or lose confidence. 
So, it is good for children to explore different jobs, but career planning should be flexible and pressure-free. 
 
Question 4: Should children ask their teachers or parents for advice when making plans? 
解题思路：对比与观点类问题。可以从“父母与老师角色不同”入手，解释父母更了解孩子的性格和生活，而
老师更熟悉学习和发展路径。重点不是二选一，而是说明在不同情境下应向不同的人求助。 
Sample Answer: 
I think children should ask both teachers and parents for advice when making plans. Parents know their 
children very well. They understand their personality, habits, and daily life, so their advice is often caring and 
supportive. 
Teachers, on the other hand, have experience in education and planning for the future. They can give useful 
advice about study goals, skills, and learning paths.

喜欢画画的孩子 
Describe a child who loves drawing or painting 
You should say: 
Who he or she is 
How or when you knew him or her 
How often he or she draws or paints 
And explain why you think he or she loves drawing or painting 
 
教师的解析： 
审题方面 
这是一道“人物类 + 兴趣爱好类”话题，要求你描述一位非常喜欢画画或绘画的孩子。题目需要你说清：这个孩子
是谁、你是如何或在什么时候认识他或她的、他或她画画的频率，以及你为什么认为他或她真的热爱画画。整体以
现在时描述习惯，用过去时补充认识经过，重点在于持续行为和内在动机。 
思路方面 
这里可以选择你现实中接触过的孩子，比如朋友的孩子或社区里的小朋友。思路上可以先说清你是通过什么场合认
识这个孩子的；然后要说清他或她多久画一次画；接着可以重点说你观察到的细节，比如是否主动画画、是否专
注、是否从中获得快乐；最后总结你为什么觉得这是真正的热爱。 
结构方面 
第一段：介绍这个孩子是谁，以及你是如何或什么时候认识他或她的；第二段：描述他或她画画的频率、内容和日
常表现；第三段：解释你为什么认为他或她热爱画画，并表达你的感受。 
 
答题参考 
背景描述： 
The child is my friend’s son. 这个孩子是我朋友的儿子。 
I met him at a community center. 我是在社区活动中心认识他的。 
He is around seven years old. 他大约七岁。 
He is friendly and imaginative. 他很友好，也很有想象力。 
Drawing is his favorite activity. 画画是他最喜欢的活动。 
具体描述 
He draws almost every evening. 他几乎每天晚上都会画画。 
He likes using colored pencils. 他喜欢用彩铅。 
He often draws stories he imagines. 他经常画自己想象的故事。 
He can sit and draw for a long time. 他可以坐着画很久。 
He enjoys talking about his drawings. 他喜欢讲自己的画。 
描述感受 
Drawing helps him express ideas. 画画帮助他表达想法。 
It makes him feel relaxed and happy. 这让他感到放松和快乐。 
I can see his focus and patience. 我能看到他的专注和耐心。 
He draws for enjoyment, not results. 他画画是为了享受过程。 
That is why I believe he loves drawing. 这就是我认为他热爱画画的原因。 
 
Sample Answer  ： 
A child who loves drawing is my friend’s son, who is about seven years old. I first met him last year at a 
community center where several families often gather on weekends. At that time, I noticed that while other 
children were running around, he was sitting quietly at a table with paper and pencils. That was how I first 
realized his strong interest in drawing. 
He draws almost every evening after finishing his homework. According to his parents, drawing has become 
part of his daily routine. He likes using colored pencils and often draws animals, cartoon characters, and small 
stories from his imagination.

Part 3  
Question 1: What is the right age for a child to learn drawing? 
解题思路：观点 + 原因类问题。重点不是给“唯一年龄”，而是解释为什么幼儿阶段适合开始。 
Sample Answer: 
I think children can start learning drawing at a young age, around four or five. At this age, children enjoy colors 
and shapes and like to express ideas freely. 
Drawing helps them develop creativity and hand skills. It should be fun, not stressful. There is no need for strict 
rules at first. 
So, early drawing is good as long as it is relaxed and enjoyable. 
 
Question 2: Why do most children draw more often than adults do? 
解题思路：原因类问题。可以从“时间”“压力”“表达方式”解释为什么儿童更常画画。 
Sample Answer: 
Most children draw more often because they have more free time and fewer worries. Drawing is a natural way 
for children to express ideas and feelings. They enjoy using colors and imagination without thinking too much 
about results. 
Adults are usually busy with work and family, so they have less time. They may also feel shy or think they are 
not good at drawing. 
So, children draw more because drawing feels easy, fun, and stress-free for them. 
 
Question 3: Why do some people visit galleries or museums instead of viewing artworks online? 
解题思路：原因类问题。重点解释“现场体验”的价值，并对比线上观看的局限。 
Sample Answer: 
Some people visit galleries or museums because they want a real experience. Seeing artworks in person helps 
people feel the size, color, and details better. 
Museums also offer a quiet and special environment, which helps people focus and learn. Online viewing is 
convenient, but it cannot replace the feeling of being there. 
So, people visit museums to enjoy art more deeply and seriously. 
 
Question 4: Do you think galleries and museums should be free of charge? 
解题思路：观点类问题。可以采取“部分免费或低价”的平衡立场，解释文化公平与运营成本之间的关系。 
Sample Answer: 
I think galleries and museums should be free or low-cost. Free entry allows more people, especially children 
and low-income families, to learn about culture and history. It helps education and social equality. 
However, museums need money to protect artworks and run programs. Without income, quality may drop. 
So, free entry on certain days or lower prices are a good balance between access and management.

想见的名人 
Describe a famous person you would like to meet 
You should say: 
Who he or she is 
How you knew him or her 
How or where you would like to meet him or her 
And explain why you would like to meet him or her 
 
教师的解析： 
审题方面 
这是一道“人物类 + 个人愿望类”话题，要求你描述一位你想见面的名人。需要说明：他或她是谁、你是如何认识
或了解到他或她的、你希望在什么场合或地点见面，以及你为什么想见他或她。整体以现在时和将来时为主，介绍
认识过程时可用过去时。重点在于个人原因和情感动机。 
思路方面 
这里可以选择歌手、演员、运动员或作家。思路上可以先说清这个人是谁，以及你最早是通过什么方式知道他的；
然后可以说一说你希望在哪里、以什么方式见到他；最后可以重点说你想见他的原因，比如受到启发、喜欢他的作
品或性格。 
结构方面 
第一段：介绍这位名人是谁，以及你是如何认识他的； 
第二段：说明你希望在什么地方、什么情境下见到他； 
第三段：解释你为什么想见他，并总结你的感受。 
 
答题参考

He is well known for his talent and style. 他因才华和风格而出名 
具体描述 
I would like to meet him at a music event. 我希望在音乐活动上见到他。 
It would be great to talk with him face to face. 如果能面对面交流会很棒。 
I want to tell him how his work influenced me. 我想告诉他他的作品对我的影响。 
I imagine the meeting would be short but meaningful. 我想象这次见面会很短但很有意义。 
I would feel nervous but excited. 我会既紧张又兴奋。 
描述感受 
I admire his creativity and hard work. 我钦佩他的创造力和努力。 
He inspired me during my teenage years. 他在我青少年时期给了我启发。 
Meeting him would be a dream come true. 见到他将是梦想成真。 
I believe the experience would motivate me. 我相信这次经历会激励我。 
That is why I really want to meet him. 这就是我很想见他的原因。

Part 3  
Question 1: What are the advantages and disadvantages of being a famous child? 
解题思路：评估类问题。需要同时解释好处和问题。 
Sample Answer: 
Being a famous child has advantages. They get more chances, money, and attention. They may start a career 
early. 
But there are disadvantages too. Famous children have less privacy and more pressure. They may lose a normal 
childhood. 
So, fame brings opportunities, but it also brings stress and responsibility. 
 
Question 2: What can today’s children do to become famous? 
解题思路：说明类问题。可以从网络平台、才艺展示、内容创作展开。 
Sample Answer: 
Today’s children can become famous through the internet. They can share talents like singing, drawing, or 
sports online. Social media gives them a large audience. 
Some also join talent shows or competitions. However, fame should not be the main goal. Children still need 
education and guidance. 
So, technology offers chances, but fame should come naturally and safely. 
 
Question 3: What can children do with their fame? 
解题思路：说明 + 评估类问题。可以说明积极用途，同时提醒风险。 
Sample Answer: 
Children can use fame to share positive messages and inspire others. They may support good causes or show 
their talents. 
However, fame also brings pressure. Children need guidance to handle attention and protect privacy. 
So, fame can be useful, but children need support to use it wisely. 
 
Question 4: Do people become famous because of their talent? 
解题思路：原因 + 评估类问题。可以说明“天赋重要但不唯一”。 
Sample Answer: 
Talent helps people become famous, but it is not the only reason. People with strong skills in music, sports, or 
acting often get attention. 
However, luck, timing, and social media also matter. Some talented people are not famous, while others gain 
fame quickly online. 
So, talent is important, but success depends on many factors.

鼓励你保护自然的人 
Describe a person who encouraged you to protect the nature 
You should say: 
Who he or she is 
How he or she encouraged you 
What he or she encouraged you to do 
And explain how you feel about this person 
 
教师的解析： 
审题方面 
这是一道“人物类 + 环保意识类”话题，要求你描述一位鼓励你保护自然的人。需要说明：他或她是谁、他或她是
如何鼓励你的、具体鼓励你做了什么，以及你对这个人的感受。整体以过去时叙述经历，用现在时总结影响和感
受。重点在于影响方式和个人改变。 
思路方面 
这里可以选择老师、家人或朋友。思路上可以先说清这个人是谁以及你们的关系；然后可以说一说他或她通过哪些
方式鼓励你，比如言语、行动或活动；接着重点可以放在他或她具体让你做了哪些环保行为；最后说明你对这个人
的态度以及这件事对你的长期影响。 
结构方面 
第一段：介绍这个人是谁，以及你是如何认识他的； 
第二段：描述他或她如何鼓励你，并让你做了哪些事情； 
第三段：解释你对这个人的感受，以及这次经历的意义。 
 
答题参考 
背景描述： 
The person is my biology teacher. 这个人是我的生物老师。 
I met him during high school. 我是在高中认识他的。 
He cares deeply about the environment. 他非常关心环境。 
He often talked about nature in class. 他经常在课堂上谈到自然。 
He influenced me in a positive way. 他对我产生了积极影响。 
具体描述 
He encouraged us to reduce waste. 他鼓励我们减少浪费。 
He asked us to use reusable items. 他让我们使用可重复使用的物品。 
He organized a clean-up activity. 他组织了一次清理活动。 
He led by example. 他以身作则。 
He explained why small actions matter. 他解释了小行动的重要性。 
描述感受 
I feel grateful for his guidance. 我很感激他的引导。 
He changed the way I think about nature. 他改变了我对自然的看法。 
I respect him a lot. 我非常尊敬他。 
His words still influence me today. 他的话至今仍影响着我。 
That is why he means a lot to me. 这就是他对我很重要的原因。 
 
Sample Answer  ： 
A person who encouraged me to protect nature is my biology teacher from high school. I met him when I was 
around sixteen years old, and he left a strong impression on me because he truly cared about the environment. 
He did not only teach from textbooks, but also shared his own actions and ideas. 
He encouraged me by explaining environmental problems in a simple and clear way. In class, he often talked 
about pollution, waste, and climate change, and he helped us understand how daily habits can affect nature. 
He encouraged us to reduce waste, use reusable bags and bottles, and save water and electricity at home. He 
also organized a clean-up activity in a nearby park, where we picked up rubbish together. By taking part in this

Part 3  
Question 1: How can parents teach their children to protect nature? 
解题思路：解决方式类问题。可以从“以身作则”“日常小行为”“亲身体验”三个角度展开，重点解释父母如
何通过生活中的具体做法影响孩子，而不是只停留在说教层面。 
Sample Answer: 
Parents can teach children to protect nature by setting a good example. When children see parents saving 
water, sorting rubbish, or using fewer plastic bags, they learn naturally. 
Parents can also explain simple ideas, such as why trees are important or why animals need protection. Taking 
children to parks or nature areas helps them feel close to the environment. 
Small actions, like not wasting food and keeping places clean, make children understand responsibility. 
So, through daily habits, clear explanations, and real experiences, parents can help children care about nature. 
 
Question 2: Should schools teach children to get close to nature? 
解题思路：观点类问题。可以从“身心发展”“环境意识”“学习方式多样化”三个角度展开，解释学校为什么
有责任引导孩子亲近自然，而不仅是完成课堂教学。 
Sample Answer: 
Yes, I think schools should teach children to get close to nature. Nature helps children relax and stay healthy, 
especially when they spend a lot of time indoors. Being outside can reduce stress and improve mood. 
Schools can also use nature to teach students about the environment. When children see plants, animals, and 
natural spaces, they understand why protection is important. Learning outside makes lessons more interesting 
and real. 
So, helping children get close to nature supports both their health and their learning. 
 
Question 3: Do you think there should be laws to protect nature? 
解题思路：观点类问题。可以从“个人行为不够”“法律约束力强”“保护公共利益”三个角度展开，解释为什
么仅靠自觉不够，需要法律来规范行为并长期保护自然环境。 
Sample Answer: 
Yes, I think there should be laws to protect nature. Not everyone chooses to protect the environment by 
themselves, especially when it costs time or money. Laws can guide people to act responsibly. 
For example, rules about pollution, waste, and wildlife protection help reduce damage to nature. Laws also 
make companies more careful about how they use natural resources. 
So, laws are important because they protect nature in a fair and long-term way, not just by personal choice.

机智解决问题的人 
Describe a person who solved a problem in a smart way 
You should say: 
Who this person is 
What the problem was 
How he or she solved it 
And explain why you think he or she did it in a smart way 
 
教师的解析： 
审题方面 
这是一道“人物类 + 问题解决类”话题，要求你描述一位用聪明方式解决问题的人。需要说明：这个人是谁、遇到
了什么问题、他或她是如何解决的，以及你为什么认为这种方式很聪明。整体以过去时叙述事件，用现在时总结评
价。重点在于解决思路本身，而不是问题有多复杂。
